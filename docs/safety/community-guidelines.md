# OpenMatch Community Guidelines

OpenMatch is for adults looking for connection. These guidelines describe what's allowed and what isn't, and what we'll do when something crosses the line.

## You must be

- **18 or older.** No exceptions. Underage accounts are removed and may be reported to authorities.
- **Yourself.** Your profile must represent you. No impersonation, no stolen photos, no fictional characters as primary identity.

## Not allowed

Profiles or messages that include:

- **Harassment** — repeated unwanted contact, slurs, sexual remarks after the other person has declined, threats.
- **Hate or discrimination** — content that targets people for their race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.
- **Threats or violence** — credible threats, glorification of violence, calls to harm.
- **Sexual content** — explicit images or graphic sexual content in profiles or unsolicited messages. (Consensual conversation between matched adults is between them.)
- **Scams and spam** — financial scams, romance scams, "I'm stuck abroad" patterns, off-platform monetization solicitation (sugar relationships exchanged for money, cam-site promotion, OnlyFans-style pitches).
- **Fake or duplicate profiles** — bot accounts, stolen photos, the same person operating multiple active profiles.
- **Off-platform contact pushes early** — pressuring someone to move to a private channel before establishing comfort. This is a scam pattern.
- **Illegal content or activity** — drugs for sale, weapons trafficking, exploitation.

## Reporting

Anyone can report a profile, photo, or message via the in-app safety controls. Reports include:

- A reason from the standard list.
- Optional free text.
- The relevant artifact (profile, photo, or message).

Reports are reviewed by moderators. Reviewers see the reported content and the report context — not the reporter's identity, not the reported user's private fields beyond what is necessary.

## What happens after a report

In order of severity:

1. **No action** — content reviewed and within guidelines.
2. **Correction** — content removed or edited, user notified with a clear explanation.
3. **Warning** — formal warning logged to the account; another violation accelerates the response.
4. **Temporary suspension** — account disabled for a stated period.
5. **Permanent ban** — account removed; the user is barred from creating new accounts.

For threats, exploitation, and similar high-severity categories, we may move directly to suspension or ban without a warning step.

## Appeals

Banned users may appeal within **30 days** via an in-app or email appeal form. Appeals are reviewed by a moderator who was not involved in the original decision. The appeal outcome and reasoning are sent to the user.

## Blocking and unmatching

These are **always** available, always free, and always immediate:

- **Block** — two-way invisibility, removes any existing match, prevents future rediscovery, no notification to the blocked user.
- **Unmatch** — closes the conversation, stops new messages, prevents immediate rediscovery. Use this when you don't want to interact further but don't need the stronger block.

## Photo standards

- Show your face clearly in at least one photo.
- No nudity, no graphic violence, no weapons being brandished, no hate symbols.
- No images of minors as primary subject of a profile.
- No screenshots of contact info, social handles, or external payment links.
- No images of someone other than you as your primary photo.

Photos that violate these standards are removed; repeat violations lead to suspension.

## Safety practices we recommend

- Meet in public places for the first time.
- Tell a friend where you're going.
- Trust your instincts. If someone makes you uncomfortable, unmatch or block.
- We will **never** ask you for money, gift cards, or crypto. Neither will anyone genuinely using the app.

## Transparency

We publish aggregate transparency reports periodically. They include report counts by category, response time medians, action counts, and appeal outcomes. They never identify individuals.

## Crisis resources

If you are in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. The in-app safety center lists region-appropriate crisis lines.
