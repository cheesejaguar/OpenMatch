## Notice format

A notice must include all of the following or it cannot be processed:

1. A physical or electronic signature of the rights-holder or authorised agent.
2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (URL, registration, or sufficient detail).
3. Identification of the material claimed to be infringing, with enough information for us to locate it (the OpenMatch URL, user handle, and photo identifier where possible).
4. Your contact information (name, address, telephone, email).
5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
6. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorised to act on behalf of the owner.

Send notices to **dmca@openmatch.app** or the postal address above.

## What happens after a valid notice

1. We will remove or disable access to the material expeditiously.
2. We will notify the user who posted the material and forward the notice (with personal contact details redacted as appropriate).
3. We will record the notice for our repeat-infringer policy.

## Counter-notice

A user who believes the takedown was a mistake or misidentification may send a counter-notice with:

1. Their physical or electronic signature.
2. Identification of the removed material and the location where it appeared before removal.
3. A statement under penalty of perjury that the user has a good-faith belief the material was removed by mistake or misidentification.
4. The user's name, address, telephone, and a statement consenting to the jurisdiction of the federal district court for their judicial district (or, if outside the U.S., the Northern District of California).

Send counter-notices to **dmca@openmatch.app**.

If we receive a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to the original complainant. If the complainant does not file an action seeking a court order against the user within **10–14 business days**, we may restore the material.

## Repeat infringers

We terminate the accounts of repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances. Tracked in the moderation system; an account that accumulates a substantive number of unrebutted infringement claims will be terminated.

## Knowingly false notices

Misrepresentations in a DMCA notice or counter-notice may subject the sender to liability for damages, costs, and attorneys' fees under §512(f).

## Rights-holders outside the United States

We process equivalent claims under local law (e.g., EU Copyright Directive, UK CDPA, etc.). The same email — **dmca@openmatch.app** — receives non-US claims.
